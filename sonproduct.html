What is an Bat Trang ceramic vase?

Bat Trang ceramics is the common name of Vietnamese ceramics produced in Bat Trang village, Bat Trang commune, Gia Lam district, Hanoi.[1][2] In the Sino-Vietnamese sense, the word Bat (鉢) is the monk's eating bowl (in Sanskrit, Patra), the word Trang (場, also read as Truong) means "big yard", a piece of land reserved for expertise. [3] Thus, the name Bat Trang can be understood as "a large yard specializing in producing bowls". However, according to some other documents, the name Bat Trang may originate from the name Bach Tho Phuong, meaning "white clay ward".
